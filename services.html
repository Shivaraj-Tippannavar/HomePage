Medical Services

  • Primary Care: General medical services provided by healthcare providers such as family physicians, pediatricians, and internists, including routine check-ups, screenings, vaccinations, and treatment of common illnesses.
  • Specialty Care: Specialized medical services provided by healthcare specialists, such as cardiologists, neurologists, oncologists, dermatologists, and others, focusing on specific health conditions or organ systems.
  • Emergency Care: Immediate medical attention provided in emergency situations for severe injuries, acute illnesses, or life-threatening conditions, often delivered in hospital emergency departments or urgent care centers.
  • Surgical Services: Medical procedures involving incisions, excisions, suturing, and other interventions performed by surgeons to treat injuries, diseases, or conditions, ranging from minor surgeries to complex operations.
  • Diagnostic Services: Medical tests, imaging studies, and laboratory analyses used to diagnose diseases, monitor health conditions, and guide treatment decisions, including blood tests, X-rays, MRIs, CT scans, ultrasounds, and biopsies.
  • Mental Health Services: Assessment, counseling, therapy, and medication management provided by psychiatrists, psychologists, therapists, and counselors to address mental health disorders, emotional issues, and behavioral challenges.
  • Rehabilitation Services: Physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, and other rehabilitation interventions aimed at restoring function, mobility, and independence following illness, injury, or surgery.
  • Preventive Care: Health promotion, disease prevention, and wellness services focused on maintaining optimal health and preventing illnesses, including health education, screenings, vaccinations, and lifestyle counseling.
  • Maternity and Obstetric Care: Prenatal care, childbirth services, postnatal care, and reproductive health services provided by obstetricians, midwives, and other maternal healthcare providers to support women throughout pregnancy, labor, and delivery.
  • Geriatric Care: Healthcare services tailored to the unique needs of older adults, including preventive care, management of chronic conditions, rehabilitation, and support services to promote healthy aging and quality of life.
